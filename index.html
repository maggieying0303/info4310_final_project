Comparing Top Movie Streaming Platforms

Netflix & Chill or Prime & Fun Time? 🤔

Streaming video-on-demand has seen a significant rise in the U.S. in recent years, and is projected to become an even larger market in the coming years. The global video streaming market size was valued at USD 42.60 billion in 2019 and is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 20.4% from 2020 to 2027.

In these visualizations, we explore how movie content compares across 4 of the most popular video-on-demand streaming platforms worldwide.

Through examining factors such as genre, ratings, cost, and more, we hope to provide you with a better understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each platform, and help movie lovers make a decision about which platform best suits their needs and interests.



#1. genre availability across platforms

Prime Video wins the number game, on account of volume alone.

Across all of 8 of the most popular genres, Prime Video dominates in terms of rough numbers, given the sheer number of movies it has licensed out on its platform.

Interestingly, despite having the smallest selection of movies compared to other platforms, Disney+ does well in the Family genre, overtaking Netflix & Hulu and coming comparably close to Prime Video.




#2. which platforms dominate which genres?

If you're looking for a Thrill, Disney+ may not be right for you.

But raw numbers aren't telling the full stories. Proportionately speaking, it's clear that certain platforms specialize in delivering certain genres. Disney+, for example, counts 46% of its total repertoire as Adventure movies, and 76% as Family movies (many of these titles are cross-listed under multiple genres).

As you toggle through the genre filters, you might find that Netflix, Hulu, and Prime Video have similar composition of genres. For people who consistently have a favorite genre in mind, understanding where each of these platforms specialize can help inform decisions about which to subscribe to.

Despite having the largest volume of movies available, the ratings of movies on Prime Video are overall much lower than other platforms.

For nearly every genre, Prime Video is outperformed by its counterparts, excepting a few like Documentaries where it outperforms Netflix. Disney+, in contrast, demonstrates a strong performance in multiple genres, proving that it has a rather high-quality repertoire despite of its lack in volume.
